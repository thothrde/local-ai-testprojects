TDA on L-function Zeros Topologische Datenanalyse auf Nullstellen von L-Funktionen

Phase 7 complete · n=10,000 validated · honest self-correction PARI/GP 2.17 · GUDHI 3.11 · ripser 0.6 · Mac Studio M3 Ultra 256 GB n = 6,086 · |D| ≤ 10,000 · k = 8

Idea

The zeros of Dirichlet L-functions follow random-matrix statistics, according to the Montgomery–Odlyzko law and the Katz–Sarnak program. For real Dirichlet characters (Kronecker symbols χD), Katz–Sarnak predicts orthogonal symmetry: the local zero-spacing statistics should follow the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE).

This project asks a different question: does this also hold for the global topology of an entire L-function family, as measured by persistent homology (TDA, Vietoris–Rips)? Each L-function L(s,χD) is represented by its first k Katz–Sarnak-normalised zeros as a point in ℝk; the point cloud of the whole family is then analysed topologically.

Main Finding (final, after Phase 7)

Triply confirmed, cross-family validated result: The global H₀ topology of L-function point clouds deviates significantly from genuine GOE/GUE random matrices. Confirmed for Dirichlet characters (n=6,086, z = −20.97) AND independently for elliptic curves (n=1,100, z = −19.56, degree 2 instead of degree 1). A completely model-free permutation test with n=10,000 (the highest precision reached) confirms: none of the 10,000 random permutations reached the observed parity deviation (p < 1/10,001).
Important caveat: This is not a refutation of classical Katz–Sarnak theory (n-level density, local spacing statistics) — that theory is in fact confirmed by our own validity check (the Atas ⟨r⟩ statistic of the generated matrices matches the literature values exactly). The deviation concerns a specific TDA statistic (the sum of H₀ persistence lifetimes of a Vietoris–Rips filtration), which is not known to be equivalent to classical RMT quantities.
Equally important — a correction: A class-number correlation found in Phase 6 was refuted in Phase 7 by our own replication on independent data (cause: a coding bug in the original analysis). This project documents both confirmed and refuted findings — transparency about self-correction is part of the methodology, not its opposite.

Phase 6: Three Extensions

Phase 6 tests the Phase-5 finding in three independent ways: correlation with arithmetic invariants, a tenfold larger permutation test with stored raw data, and a completely independent test on a second L-function family (elliptic curves, degree 2 instead of degree 1).

6C — Cross-Family Test: Elliptic Curves

300 elliptic curves (systematically generated from small Weierstrass coefficients, conductor 11–4,000) were analysed with methodology identical to Phase 5: genuine GOE matrices, matched null (conductor + root number instead of conductor + parity), self-consistency test. The root number w=ellrootno(E) takes over the role of the Dirichlet parity: w=−1 forces a zero at γ=0, exactly as for odd characters.

Phase 7: Precision Extension and an Honest Self-Correction

Phase 7 implements the three adversarial recommendations from Phase 6: a permutation test twenty times larger (n=10,000, via 20 parallel processes on the Mac Studio's 32 cores), an independent replication of the class-number correlation on new discriminants, and an elliptic-curve family nearly four times bigger (n=1,100 instead of 300). In the process, a bug was discovered and fixed in the original replication — with an important, uncomfortable result.

7B — Replication of the Class-Number Correlation: FAILED

An important self-correction: The first Phase-7 run contained a coding bug — the PARI/GP script only covered positive discriminants (for(dv=10001,20000,...)), omitting every odd character. After correction (a separate run for negative D), the picture is clear: the Phase-6 correlation does NOT replicate.

The central methodological lesson between Phase 4 and Phase 5: Phase 4 compared against a Wigner–Dyson-surmise approximation (nearest-neighbour statistics only) and inadvertently used an external literature value (~0.531, the Atas ⟨r⟩ statistic) as a reference — a different quantity from our H₀ ratio. Phase 5 replaces WDS with genuine diagonalised random matrices and establishes the reference empirically via self-consistency (two independent null clones tested against each other).

Permutation Test in Detail

Observed ratio of H₀ persistence sums between odd (D<0) and even (D>0) characters: 0.8551. If instead 80× random 3,043/3,043 subsets are drawn from the ENTIRE point cloud (disregarding true parity), the mean comes out at 1.0004 ± 0.0064, CI95=[0.9889, 1.0121]. None of the 80 permutations came close to 0.8551. This test requires no RMT model at all — it shows directly that the parity information is itself topologically encoded in the data.

Correlation r=−0.325: r² ≈ 0.106 explains only ~10.6% of the variance. The pooled correlation reflects group weighting (odd vs. even) more than a uniform arithmetic law — the odd effect alone is weak (r=−0.122), the even effect is practically zero (p=0.87).

What Stands Robustly After 7 Phases

The one finding that has survived every replication, every adversarial test, and every increase in sample size: the global H₀ topology of L-function families systematically deviates from genuine GOE/GUE random matrices, confirmed independently for Dirichlet characters (degree 1, n=6,086, z=−20.97) AND for elliptic curves (degree 2, n=1,100, z=−19.56). The class-number-correlation hypothesis from Phase 6 was refuted by our own replication — an example of how early, significant results in complex pipelines can be technical artefacts, and why independent replication before any conclusion is mandatory.

Open Questions

Does the H₀ persistence-sum statistic have a known relationship to classical RMT quantities (spectral rigidity, number variance), or is it a genuinely new invariant? Does the cross-family effect scale with the degree of the L-function, or is it family-specific? A degree-dependent convergence analysis (degree 1, 2, 3...) would be the next step.

Technical Details

Tools: PARI/GP 2.17.3 (lfunzeros, 15 decimal digits), GUDHI 3.11.0 (H0, max_dim=1), ripser 0.6.15 (H1), NumPy/SciPy (matrix diagonalisation via eigvalsh, Accelerate BLAS), qwen3:235b-a22b + qwen3.6:35b-a3b (Ollama, local). Mac Studio M3 Ultra, 256 GB.

Phase 5 methodology: 60×2 genuine GOE/GUE matrices (N=1,200) diagonalised, empirical polynomial unfolding (degree 12, 70% bulk), sequential window extraction to preserve genuine joint correlations, matched null per L-function (same conductor + parity), self-consistency test (two independent null clones), bootstrap CI (100×, n=600), permutation test (80×, model-free).

Phase 5 runtime: approx. 11 minutes (matrix generation, TDA, bootstrap, permutation, committee).

Phase 7 methodology: permutation test parallelised across 20 worker processes (32-core Mac Studio M3 Ultra), independent replication on |D|∈(10,000,20,000], elliptic-curve family widened to n=1,100 via a broader Weierstrass-coefficient search (conductor up to 8,000).

Phase 7 runtime: approx. 43 minutes for the main run (permutation test n=10,000 dominates at ~39 minutes), plus a short supplementary PARI/GP run to fix the replication bug.

Scripts: tda_phase5.py, tda_phase6.py, tda_phase7.py, tda_phase7b_fix.py — all under /Volumes/CLAUDE-DATA/TDA-Project/ on the local Mac Studio.
