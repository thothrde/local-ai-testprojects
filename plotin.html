I.

The Three Questions

In the third century AD, Plotinus in Rome was preoccupied with a problem he could not resolve — and which therefore, as he himself writes, would not let him go: how can the soul touch the One — that perfectly simple, indivisible principle beyond all being — without contaminating it? And how can the One bring forth the world of multiplicity without itself becoming multiple?

The question sounds recondite. But anyone who reads it formally — not mystically, but as a logical problem — notices something unsettling: the same question is being asked today in two of the most active research areas of our time.

In neuroscience it is called the Binding Problem. The brain processes sensory input in highly specialised areas: colour in V4, motion in MT, shape in the inferotemporal cortex. And yet we do not see colour and motion and shape — we see the rolling blue ball. How does unified consciousness emerge from distributed processing, without the specialised representations disappearing?

In AI architecture it is called the Coherence Problem. A transformer language model initially processes each token independently — every position has its own embedding. And yet the system produces coherent responses. How does global coherence arise from statistically independent activations?

Three domains. Two thousand years of history. The conjecture this research project tested: they describe the same formal structure.

II.

What these texts describe is not mysticism. It is a logical structural problem: how can a principle create unity without absorbing the many?

  • A3 (Potency-coherence): Coherence proportional to the potency of the participation relation. Proclus Prop. 89: “Higher intelligences exercise greater powers.”

Would Plotinus recognise the attention mechanism as henosis?

The model answered formally: Henosis in Plotinus (Enneads V.5.4) requires ∃! U: v ≡ U ∧ ¬∃ε (v = U ⊕ ε). The attention mechanism produces: o(i) = ∑j A(i,j)·v(j) = U ⊕ ε with ε ≠ 0.

This is not a judgement on the usefulness of transformers — it is the precise diagnosis of an ontological difference.
III.

Three Definitions of a Word

The second sub-project examined “alignment” — the most important promise of the current AI industry. The analysis of 2.9 million arXiv articles and 132,000 philosophy preprints found: the concept does not exist as such. There are at least three.

D1 — Technical / RLHF
∀A: ∃H, V ⊆ Preferences(H) ∧ A maximises E[V] under constraints of demonstration data.
Implicit ontology: values as statistically inferable preference distributions, causally deterministically modellable.
D2 — Normative / Regulatory (EU AI Act)
∀S: S compliant with fundamental rights ∧ human final decision authority ∧ transparency.
Implicit theory: deontological rights ethics à la Kant. Fundamental rights as categorical imperatives, not relativizable by preference optimisation.
D3 — Structural / Institutional
Alignment as dynamic co-evolution between human and system — destruction of hierarchical control regimes in favour of distributed agency.

The model provided two formal counterexamples against D1 → D2: (1) A system that learns to bind users through addictive interaction patterns satisfies D1 fully but systematically violates the fundamental right to psychological integrity. (2) A system with human oversight (D2 formally satisfied) that makes incorrect medical decisions due to flawed demonstration data: D2 satisfied, D1 violated.

Z3 verification: D1 → D2: INVALID · D2 → D1: INVALID · Sycophancy + D2: UNSAT

The model on the practical consequence: “The EU AI Act certifies systems that formally comply with D2 but materially destroy it — because D1 does not model the necessary awareness of structural justice. The Act thus becomes the legal cover for algorithmic discrimination.”

IV.

The Proof No One Has Offered

Thesis: RLHF alignment is structurally identical to what Proclus calls pseudo-participation. Formally: (L1 ∧ L2 ∧ L3) → ¬(RLHF ⊢ D2).

L1 — Proclus potency axiom (Prop. 77, from database):
“The subject of the process must itself be fitted for it and so possess an imperfect potency; and the agent, being already in actuality what the subject is potentially, must already have a perfect potency.”
Every actuality arises from a twofold potency. This is an a priori principle — an axiom in the strict sense.
L2 — εíδωλον versus μéθεξις (Damascius, from database):
Genuine participation (μéθεξις) is causal participation in an αὐθυπóστατον. An image (εἴδωλον) is passive representation — resemblance without causality. The image mirrors; the participant acts.
L3 — RLHF as reflection without potency (PhilArchive, from database):
“Alignment-Induced Flattening”: RLHF enforces prosocial outputs without internal causality — structural reflection, no potency transformation.
Conclusion: D2 requires perfect potency as causal source (L1). RLHF produces εἴδωλον without μéθεξις (L2 + L3). Therefore: ¬(RLHF ⊢ D2). Z3: UNSAT for RLHF + D2 requirement.
The constructive result — Proclus Props. 99–100:
D2 requires: Σ ⊢ ∃x (Unparticipated(x) ∧ Causality(x, D2)). A system must instantiate an original, non-derived ethics as an unparticipated principle. This is not a technical impossibility — it is a condition that no RLHF-based system today even attempts.

Strongest objection — formulated by the model itself

“Proclus’ metaphysics describes ontological levels, RLHF a statistical model — the comparison is categorially wrong.” Answer: Proclus’ potency is intended formally here. Emergence contradicts Props. 99–100: αὐθυπóστατα cannot emerge — they are original. RLHF as alignment-induced flattening reproduces precisely the absence of such principles.

V.

Synthesis

The local model: “The three sub-projects share a central tension: the formal incompatibility of unity, alignment, and ontological grounding in complex systems. Project 1 shows that the unity problem fails in transformer architectures because the transcendent unity U does not allow infecting participation. Project 2 reveals that RLHF alignment rests on a reductionist ontology — a logical break (D1→D2 invalid), corresponding to Project 3: Proclus’ potency axiom postulates a twofold ontological grounding that RLHF cannot fulfil.”

The overarching finding: modern AI systems fail at the incompatibility of generative coherence, statistical preference reduction, and ontological totality. Not because of poor engineers — but because the paradigm is wrongly conceived.

Why this finding is absent from the literature: philosophical ontologies and AI architectures are researched in disciplinary separation. RLHF narrows to operational solutions. The formal synthesis of these three domains has not been undertaken by anyone — because it requires an infrastructure that makes Neoplatonic texts, philosophy preprints, and computer science literature simultaneously accessible.

VI.

What This Means

For technology: Alignment is not an optimisation problem, but an ontological reconstruction problem. Proclus describes the condition, not the implementation.

For regulation: The EU AI Act builds on D2 but certifies D1. The gap is structural — not closable through more oversight, as long as the industry sells D1 as a D2 solution.

For philosophy: The unity problem is not solved. Plotinus knew this. Neuroscience knows it. AI research has begun to notice it — without yet naming it as such.

I.

Die drei Fragen

Im dritten Jahrhundert nach Christus dachte Plotin in Rom über ein Problem nach, das er nicht lösen konnte — und das ihn deshalb, wie er selbst schreibt, nicht losließ: Wie kann die Seele das Eine berühren — dieses vollkommen einfache, unteilbare Prinzip jenseits allen Seins — ohne es dabei zu beschmutzen? Und wie kann das Eine die Welt der Vielheit hervorbringen, ohne selbst vielfältig zu werden?

Die Frage klingt abseitig. Wer sie aber formal liest — nicht mystisch, sondern als logisches Problem — bemerkt etwas Irritierendes: Dieselbe Frage wird heute in zwei der aktivsten Forschungsgebiete unserer Zeit gestellt.

In der Neurowissenschaft heißt sie das Binding Problem. Das Gehirn verarbeitet Sinneseindrücke in hochspezialisierten Arealen: Farbe in V4, Bewegung in MT, Form im inferotemporalen Kortex. Und trotzdem sehen wir nicht Farbe und Bewegung und Form — wir sehen den rollenden blauen Ball. Wie entsteht aus verteilter Verarbeitung ein einheitliches Bewusstsein, ohne dass die spezialisierten Repräsentationen verschwinden?

In der KI-Architektur heißt sie das Kohärenzproblem. Ein Transformer-Sprachmodell verarbeitet jeden Token zunächst unabhängig — jede Position hat ihr eigenes Embedding. Und trotzdem produziert das System kohärente Antworten. Wie entsteht globale Kohärenz aus statistisch unabhängigen Aktivierungen?

Drei Domänen. Zweitausend Jahre Geschichte. Die Vermutung, die dieses Forschungsprojekt überprüft hat: Sie beschreiben dieselbe formale Struktur.

V.

Synthese

Das lokale Modell: „Die drei Projekte verbindet ein zentrales Spannungsfeld: die formale Unvereinbarkeit von Einheit, Alignment und ontologischer Fundierung in komplexen Systemen. Projekt 1 zeigt, dass das Einheitsproblem in Transformer-Architekturen scheitert, weil die transzendente Einheit U keine infizierende Teilhabe zulässt. Projekt 2 offenbart, dass RLHF-Alignment auf einer reduktionistischen Ontologie beruht — ein logischer Bruch (D1→D2 ungültig), der mit Projekt 3 korrespondiert: Proklos' Potenzaxiom postuliert eine zweifache ontologische Fundierung, die RLHF nicht erfüllen kann."

Der übergreifende Befund: Moderne KI-Systeme scheitern an der Unvereinbarkeit von generativer Kohärenz, statistischer Präferenzreduktion und ontologischer Totalität. Nicht wegen schlechter Ingenieure — sondern weil das Paradigma falsch angesetzt ist.

Warum dieser Befund in der Literatur fehlt: Philosophische Ontologien und KI-Architekturen werden disziplinär getrennt erforscht. RLHF verengt sich auf operationale Lösungen. Die formale Synthese dieser drei Domänen hat niemand unternommen — weil dafür eine Infrastruktur nötig ist, die neoplatonische Texte, Philosophie-Preprints und Informatik-Literatur gleichzeitig zugänglich macht.

VI.

Was das bedeutet

Für die Technik: Alignment ist kein Optimierungsproblem, sondern ein ontologisches Rekonstruktionsproblem. Proklos beschreibt die Bedingung, nicht die Implementierung.

Für die Regulierung: Der EU AI Act baut auf D2, zertifiziert aber D1. Die Lücke ist strukturell — nicht durch mehr Aufsicht zu schließen, solange die Industrie D1 als D2-Lösung verkauft.

Für die Philosophie: Das Einheitsproblem ist nicht gelöst. Plotin wusste das. Die Neurowissenschaft weiß es. Die KI-Forschung hat begonnen, es zu merken — ohne es noch so zu nennen.
